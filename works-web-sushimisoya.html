Process

Temporary Site: Due to the Covid-19, the number of customers was significantly decreased, making the client's business difficult. However, there were frequent phone calls to take out food. Since there was no website of the restaurant, he had to explain the menu one by one for his customers whenever he got a call. I immediately purchased and hosted the domain and published it with the existing JPG menu images by WordPress.

Client Interview: In an environment where face-to-face meetings are limited, I was able to figure out the client's design taste and functional requirements through Google Survey.

Concept confirmed: Visitor data was collected for a month through Google Analytics. As a result, over 78 percent of customers had accessed via mobile. Assuming that these customers were the ones with high conversion rates, who were likely to call and come to the store right away, I decided to keep the mobile version in mind first.

Collapsible menu: The biggest issue for creating a 'simple' website was that there were too many menus. As the restaurant specializes in sushi and rolls, the total number of menus was over 180. To make it as simple as possible, I came up with a JavaScript solution that can spread(open) and collapse(close) by the menu category.
